Adobe Garamond

History

Adobe Garamond, designed by Robert Slimbach in 1989, is a simplistic serif typeface that is considered to be in the roman font family. Its name roots from the French type designer and engraver Claude Garamond. Slimbach designed this typeface as a reaction to his inspiration of Robert Granjon’s italic Garamond assistant. Adobe Garamond is a highly legible typeface that reads well on print and web pages. It was later released as a Pro typeface with the OpenType feature, which can be used digitally in Adobe InDesign. It has six font options including regular, italic, semibold, semibold italic, bold, and bold italic. Its versatility and timelessness allow it to be used for anything ranging from simple articles to professional logos.

Minion

History

Minion Pro, originally also designed by Robert Slimbach in 1990, is a classical, oldstyle typeface that evokes the late Renaissance Era, a period of elegant and highly readable type designs. The Cyrillic additions were released in 1992, and the final OpenType Pro version released in 2000. The Minion Pro family has three weights and two widths, supporting many Western languages including Greek and Cyrillic. It has a handful of ligatures, small caps, oldstyle figures, swashes, and other glyphs, which makes it ideal for using in limited-edition books, newsletters, or packaging. Like Adobe Garamond Minion is highly versatile across many platforms and has excellent readability.
